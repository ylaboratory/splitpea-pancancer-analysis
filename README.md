## About
Exploring the intricate network of protein-protein interactions (PPIs) within cells is pivotal for understanding the mechanisms behind complex diseases, including cancer. Yet, conventional network-based approaches often neglect the influence of alternative splicing, a process which shapes diverse protein diversity and can impact PPIs. Our study seeks to bridge this gap by modeling PPI network rewiring using underlying domain-domain interactions and differential exon usage. Leveraging our previously developed tool, Splitpea, we perform a systematic analysis of PPI network rewiring across 28 cancer types and 7,949 patients capturing rewiring events observed in cancer relative to a normal tissue background. Through a pan-cancer analysis we find conserved, cross-cancer, cancer-specific, and subtype-specific rewiring events and link them with known cancer drivers.
